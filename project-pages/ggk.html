Goblin Knight

Whilst studying at DAE we had a course called game projects, our first project outside of Game Jams that has a mix of programmers, Artists and an audio person.
This course allowed us 10 hours of work per week to finish this project.

The Game:
You play as a collection of goblin's that when they where younger found an old storybook about a Knight looking for the holy grail. This of course lead to an infatuation with anything knight related.
This infatuation lead to them scavenging any armor they could find and stuffing themselves into it on their own quest for the holy grail.
now they stack together, fight bandits and look for the shiniest grail they can find.

In this project, I naturally stepped into more of a managerial role, as I had a bit more experience with group projects than the rest of the team.
Alongside that, I also focused on my main responsibilities as a programmer;
handling player movement, the head's automatic behavior, interactable items in the world, and the camera system.

One aspect I particularly enjoyed was developing small tools to help the artists navigate the project more easily. Since we were working in Unreal and the C++ code needed to be compiled, I created a batch file that automatically compiled any changes and then launched the project. This kept the artists out of the code environment entirely — which made everyone’s lives a little easier.
